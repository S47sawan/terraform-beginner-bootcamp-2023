Spaghetti Carbonara Recipe

Spaghetti carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish known for its rich and creamy sauce. It's simple to prepare and absolutely delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz (225g) spaghetti
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup (100g) grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 3.5 oz (100g) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Black pepper, freshly ground
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a large pot of salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve a cup of pasta cooking water, then drain the spaghetti.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano cheese, and a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper. Set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, cook the diced pancetta or guanciale over medium heat until it becomes crispy. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  4. Add the cooked and drained spaghetti to the skillet with the pancetta and garlic. Toss to combine.
  5. Remove the skillet from heat and quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta. Toss everything together, using a bit of the reserved pasta cooking water to create a creamy sauce.
  6. Serve immediately, garnished with extra Pecorino Romano cheese and freshly ground black pepper.

Enjoy your homemade spaghetti carbonara!
